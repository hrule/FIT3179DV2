NBA TRADES

Coming into the 2025-26 season, how have transactions been shaping the league?

Disclaimer: All information correct as of 19th October 2025.

With the start of the 25-26 NBA season around the corner, lets take a quick review of on NBA transactions, and what we can expect for this season.

The map on the right shows how teams involvement in trades have changed over time. It is commonly rumoured that teams avoid trading within their own conference, however multiple analyses have found no evidence for this, and as we can see, trades seem frequent, and indiscriminatory.

Each NBA season can be thought of as a fiscal year starting from July 1st, and ending June 30th. So far, for the 2025 season, which is kicking off soon, 47 teams have been involved in trades. Typically around 100 teams are involved in trades per season, so we can expect to see more movement in the future.

Some notable observations you might be able to make are that the Spurs have had relatively little involvement in trades (38) in the past 15 seasons. This may be due to their nature to develop talent, as well as their dynasty from 1999 to 2014.

On the otherhand teams like the Philadelphia 76ers, Oklahoma City Thunder, and Houston Rockets, have been incredibly active, going through several iterations of teams to build the best team possible and stay competitive.

Flipping through the East and West conference with the buttons above, you might be able to observe a common trend both team salaries and their rankings over time. With the salaries, you'll see that all teams are above the Salary Cap, which is intended to be a maximum limit on the amount that teams are able to spend, to limit wealthier teams from constructing unfair rosters. Some will even go above the Second Apron, which incurs heavy penalties.

Conference Rankings of 24-25 Top 5 Teams over 15 seasons

Even more interestingly, in the rankings chart, the success of teams are like unpredictable waves. For the Western conference, all top 5 ranking teams of last season showed a noticeable dip in rankings in the 2021 season.
This demonstrates that teams windows of high-level competition are limited. With salary, aging, and other competition being large factors.

IMPACT

Teams trade and make transactions in order to win. Taking a look back at some of the most impactful trades of last season, Jimmy Butler and Luka Doncic are some great examples.

Before the Warriors landed Jimmy Butler, they were 25W-26L (49% win rate).
However after the Jimmy Butler trade, they went 23W-8L (74% win rate) to finish the season.

Trades can alter the trajectory of a whole franchise. The Warriors after signing Jimmy Butler, signed Al Horford recently, and aim to make the Playoffs.
The Lakers are also making big moves to surround Luka Doncic with sufficient talent.

Sometimes a single trade is the difference to winning a championship.

This upcoming season, all eyes will be on the transactions that teams have made, and how they match up to expectations.

The Detroit Pistons have proved to make a large yet cheap single year leap, similar to the San Antonio Spurs. On the otherhand, other teams are 'tanking', and slimming down to prepare for future competition, such as the Nets.

Other teams with large changes in salary such as the Caveliers, Magic, and Mavericks need to win to justify their choices.
